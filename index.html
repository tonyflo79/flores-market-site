1965

The Origin

Named for Bert Flores.

Bert Flores opened his first butcher shop in Los Angeles. He came from a long line of butchers. During the Watts riots, neighborhoods burned. Bert's shop did not. He treated people in the community well, and that is why. That is the inheritance worth a restaurant's name.

His grandson, Chef Marcus Flores, named this kitchen The Flores Market in homage — to Bert, and to the butchers before him.

The Chef

From 2007 to Today.

2007 · Culinary School

First clients.

In 2007, Chef Marcus Flores enrolled in culinary school. Before graduation, two private clients hired him as their personal chef — from word-of-mouth inside the kitchen where he trained.

Patina Restaurant Group

The LA stage.

From there, he joined the Patina Restaurant Group — the group behind the Walt Disney Concert Hall dining program — and cooked through its restaurants and event operations.

The Emmys

Cooking for the stars.

Inside Patina, he worked box services at the Emmys — plated dinners delivered to the suites. That is how he learned to cook for the stars.

Today

The Flores Market.

Today, The Flores Market cooks multicourse plated dinners, Omakase counters, and sushi service for private clients and events across Los Angeles. No two menus repeat. Every kitchen he runs, he still runs.

“The shop wasn't spared by luck. It was spared by how my grandfather treated people.” — Chef Marcus Flores
